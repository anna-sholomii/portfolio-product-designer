01 Context

Joined as the first designer
during active growth.

Executive Summary - Context
Role

First and only product designer, May 2019 - April 2022. B2B EdTech SaaS, peer-to-peer student recruitment, acquired by IDP Education 2023.

Scope

0-to-1 design system, 3 user-facing platforms, continuous discovery through to shipped product - alongside engineering and growth.

Growth

50 → 100+ institutional clients during my tenure. Design function built from zero while the product was actively shipping.

The Ambassador Platform connects prospective students with current student ambassadors - a peer-to-peer channel universities use to convert enquiries into enrolments. When I joined in May 2019, the company had around 50 institutional clients and no design function. I built it from the ground up. By the time I left in April 2022, it had grown to 100+ institutions.

Over three years I ran continuous discovery, owned all design output, and established the design system the product runs on.

02 Areas of responsibility

Three platforms.
One designer.

Executive Summary - Areas of Responsibility
Platforms

Prospect-facing widget, ambassador community app, institution admin dashboard - each with a distinct user and success metric.

Approach

Kept each platform's success metric separate despite a shared component library - decisions made once propagated without rework.

Constraint

Solo designer, full-stack product work - shipping continuously alongside engineering with no design team support.

I owned end-to-end design across three distinct product areas - each with its own user, context, and success metric.

01
Prospect-facing widget
The entry point where prospective students initiate conversations with ambassadors - embedded on university websites.
  • Redesigned the conversion flow end-to-end
  • Reduced the number of steps to start a conversation
  • Improved trust signals and ambassador selection
  • Made the experience feel personal rather than transactional
Conversion 8–10% → 13–14%
02
Ambassador mobile app
Used by student ambassadors to manage conversations, post content, and track their own engagement.
  • Leaderboard system designed to drive motivation without creating anxiety
  • Content scheduling flows for story-style posts
  • Group chat UX to keep high-volume conversations manageable
03

Gave university teams visibility into what was happening across their ambassador programme. Designed to be legible without training.
  • Conversation analytics and content performance
  • Ambassador activity levels and engagement tracking
  • AI-generated topic insights on what prospective students cared about most

04 Design system

Built the foundation
from scratch.

Executive Summary - Design System
Problem

No shared visual language when joined - every screen was a one-off, no component library, no handoff process.

Solution

Atomic component library in Figma with production-state documentation - engineers could reference directly without second-guessing.

Outcome

Decisions made once propagated across 3 platforms without rework. Became the team's permanent handoff standard.

Coming in as the first designer meant there was no shared visual language, no component library, and no design/dev handoff process. I built all of it - not as an abstract system, but as the practical infrastructure the team needed to ship faster with less ambiguity.

Component library
Built in Figma - covering all core UI patterns across widget, mobile app, and admin dashboard. Named, documented, and linked to production states so engineers could reference directly.
Visual language
Standardised typography, colour, spacing, and iconography across three surfaces. Aligned the widget's consumer feel with the dashboard's enterprise readability.
"The design system wasn't a project - it was the thing that let everything else move faster."
05 Reflection

What three years
as the only designer taught me.

Executive Summary - Reflection
Insight

Being the only designer means every decision is a systems decision - you can't afford to design in isolation when you own all three platforms.

Principle

Build for the team, not just the user. Reducing engineering ambiguity at handoff is a design outcome - not an afterthought.

Growth

3 years of end-to-end ownership - research, design, advocacy, and delivery in a product-led company during active growth.

01
Being first means building the process, not just the product

The work wasn't just shipping screens - it was establishing how design fits into the company. That meant writing briefs, facilitating research sessions, running design crits with no other designers in the room, and making the case for why certain decisions needed more time.

02
Conversion metrics are honest feedback

Tracking widget conversions in Mixpanel made the widget work unusually concrete. Moving from 8–10% to 13–14% is a real signal - not a vanity metric. Working with data that close to the business outcome sharpened my instinct for what actually moves users forward.

03
Designing for three user types simultaneously requires discipline

Prospect, ambassador, and institution admin are three fundamentally different users with different goals, contexts, and mental models. Keeping those separate - and making sure a design decision for one didn't inadvertently degrade another - was the skill I grew most in this role.

04
A design system is a trust artefact, not a style guide

The component library mattered not because it enforced visual consistency, but because it gave engineering confidence they were building the right thing. Reducing ambiguity at handoff is a design problem, and the system was my solution to it.

"Three years, three platforms, one designer."
